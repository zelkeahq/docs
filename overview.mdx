## How money actually moves

Each participating institution maintains a pre-funded account with Zelkea, allowing money to move instantly between institutional balances. When a customer sends money, the funds are moved from the sender institution's Zelkea balance to the receiver institution's balance. Institutions keep full ownership of their customer relationships and never need to share sensitive or personal customer information with Zelkea.

## Two ways money moves

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Transfers" href="/transfers">
    Handle-to-handle. One sender, one receiver. Used for P2P, remittances, and institution-to-institution settlement.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Payments" href="/payments">
    Customer-to-merchant. Authorized with a one-time code. Can split a single payment across multiple recipients.
  </Card>
</Columns>

<Tip>
  If you are a financial institution **moving money on behalf of your customers**, you want Transfers. If you are **getting paid by customers** as a business, you want Payments. That's the only decision you need to make.
</Tip>

## Things to keep in mind

- **Institutions are responsible for keeping handles updated.** Handle balances, limits, and status should always reflect the institution's own records.
- **Transfers are final.** Transfers settle instantly and cannot be reversed. If funds need to be returned, a new transfer must be initiated by the party responsible for the relationship.
- **Zelkea never sees or stores sensitive banking details.** A handle does not contain an account number, card number, or wallet ID. This information remains within the institution that issued the handle.
