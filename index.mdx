Money still moves like it's the 1970s. Every country, every bank, every rail speaks a different language, and someone has to translate between all of them by hand.

Zelkea is the protocol that fixes this at the root. It gives every financial institution in the world a shared, human-readable addressing layer: instead of routing money through account numbers, card numbers, and wallet IDs, a payment moves from `malick@wallet` to `tom@bank` as simply as an email moves from one inbox to another.

The result is a single financial network instead of thousands of disconnected ones. Settlement happens in real time. Cross-border transfers stop being a special case; they're just transfers. And customer data never has to leave the institution that holds it, so privacy isn't traded away for reach.

This is the layer banking never had: one address, one API, every institution.
