Cross-border payments have traditionally meant one of two things: correspondent banking relationships, which are slow, expensive, and require pre-funded accounts across corridors, or a patchwork of local integrations, one for every country you need to reach. With Zelkea, you don't need to know whether a payment crosses borders or stays within one market. You simply make a transfer from one handle to another.

## It's just a normal transfer

Within Zelkea, a transfer is simply the movement of money from the sender institution's balance to the receiver institution's balance. The Transfer API does not distinguish between local and international transfers, because it does not need to. A transfer is just a movement of money, regardless of where it goes, always instant and at near-zero cost.

Let's take Tom as an example. Tom is travelling from Belgium to Japan and has just finished a meal at a local sushi restaurant. Tom has a EUR bank account in Belgium, while the restaurant has a JPY bank account with a local Japanese bank.

When it is time to pay, Tom simply opens his banking app, enters the restaurant's handle, the amount, and the currency. That's it.

```bash Make the transfer
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers' \
  --header 'Authorization: Bearer <API key>' \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 1e4f5a8b-6c9d-8e7f-6a5b-4c7b9e4a3c2d' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "sender": "tom@bank",
    "receiver": "sushihouse@jpybank",
    "amount": 850,
    "currency": "JPY"
  }'
```

Tom was debited instantly, and the restaurant received the funds directly into its bank account at the same time. The transaction cost Tom's bank \$0.10 and was free for both Tom and the merchant. It would have worked exactly the same way if the merchant had been a coffee shop in Belgium or a shopping mall in Melbourne.

## What currently works, and what's next

<Note>
  Zelkea currently supports **USD** and **EUR**. Currency coverage is expanding based on institution demand. See [What we support](/what-we-support) for the current list of supported countries and currencies.
</Note>

If your institution needs a currency that isn't live yet, that's useful signal for us — reach out at [support@zelkea.com](mailto:support@zelkea.com).

Under the hood, every transfer has two currencies: the source currency and the destination currency. By default, the source currency is USD, while the destination currency is the one specified in the transfer request. If the destination currency differs from the source currency, Zelkea performs the conversion using the interbank exchange rate.

## Common use cases

- **A traveler paying a local merchant abroad** — no currency exchange counter, no foreign transaction fee tacked on by a card network.
- **A worker sending remittances home** — instant settlement instead of a multi-day wait, at a fraction of what traditional remittance corridors charge.
- **A business paying an overseas supplier** — one transfer call, regardless of which country the supplier's bank is in.
