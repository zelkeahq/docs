A marketplace's hardest payments problem usually isn't collecting money. It's what happens after checkout. A single customer payment often needs to be split between multiple parties, with sellers or creators receiving their share, your commission being deducted, and funds held temporarily while payouts are processed.

Zelkea's [split payments](/payments#splitting-a-payment) handle this in a single call.

## A checkout with three vendors and a platform fee

A customer buys from two different sellers in one order, and your platform takes a small cut:

```bash Split a single payment three ways
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header 'Authorization: Bearer <API key>' \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 5a8b6c9d-8e7f-6a5b-4c7b-9e4a3c2d1e4f' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "customer": "malick@mywallet",
    "amount": 100,
    "currency": "USD",
    "code": "3M9K7L",
    "splits": [
      { "handle": "shop@ca", "amount": 90 },
      { "handle": "marketplace@us", "amount": 10 }
    ]
  }'
```

```json Response
{
  "id": "pmt_m4n7p2",
  "customer": "malick@mywallet",
  "amount": 100,
  "currency": "USD",
  "status": "completed",
  "splits": [
    { "handle": "vendor1@us", "amount": 90, "transfer_id": "tx_a1" },
    { "handle": "marketplace@us", "amount": 10, "transfer_id": "tx_a2" }
  ],
  "created_at": "2026-07-10T09:40:11Z"
}
```

Each `transfer_id` in `splits` is independently traceable — if the vendor asks "did I get paid for order #4471," you can point to the exact leg of the transaction that answers it, not just the total.

## Why this beats the traditional model

The traditional approach is to collect full payments into a platform-controlled account, then send separate payouts to each vendor later. This creates unnecessary complexity and delays that Zelkea removes:

- **No need to hold vendor funds in transit** — The split happens at checkout, with funds moving directly from the customer's institution to each involved party's institution.
- **No payout schedules for vendors** — Vendors receive their share as soon as the customer pays, rather than waiting for weekly or monthly payout batches.
