If you're building a platform where creators, freelancers, or contributors get paid — tips, subscriptions, revenue share — the hard part has never been collecting money from fans. It's been getting that money _out_ to creators who might be in a different country, banked by an institution your payout provider doesn't support.

Zelkea handles both directions with the same two calls: [Payments](/payments) to collect, [Transfers](/transfers) to pay out.

## Collecting a tip or subscription

A fan pays with their handle and a one-time code, exactly like any other checkout:

```bash Collect a tip
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 8e7f6a5b-4c7b-9e4a-3c2d-1e4f5a8b6c9d' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "customer": "fan22@wave",
    "amount": 5,
    "currency": "USD",
    "code": "P8L3Z1"
  }'
```

## Paying the creator out — same day, not next month

Most platforms batch payouts weekly or monthly because moving money to individual creators, especially across borders, is expensive and slow through traditional rails. With Zelkea, paying a creator is just a transfer to their handle:

```bash Pay out a creator
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 9e4a3c2d-1e4f-5a8b-6c9d-8e7f6a5b4c7b' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "sender": "platform@us",
    "receiver": "creator99@djamo",
    "amount": 4.50,
    "currency": "USD"
  }'
```

Note the creator is banked through a completely different institution than the platform — that's the point. Neither side needed to know or care what the other uses.

## Splitting revenue automatically

If a payment needs to be divided the moment it's collected — a platform fee plus a creator share, or a multi-creator collaboration — do it in one call instead of collecting the full amount and paying out separately:

```json Platform fee + creator share, in one transaction
{
  "customer": "fan22@wave",
  "amount": 10,
  "currency": "USD",
  "code": "P8L3Z1",
  "splits": [
    { "handle": "creator99@djamo", "amount": 8.50 },
    { "handle": "platform@us", "amount": 1.50 }
  ]
}
```

The creator gets their share instantly — no waiting on the platform to run a payout batch, and no moment where the platform is holding the creator's money in transit.

## Why same-day payouts matter here specifically

Creators, especially early on, often depend on this income directly. A payout that clears in seconds instead of sitting in a "next payout: in 12 days" queue is a real difference in a real person's week, not just a nicer number on a dashboard.
