Zelkea APIs are modular building blocks designed to create fast, flexible, and unified payment experiences. The platform allows money to move instantly across different regions and entities through a single interface.

This guide outlines the core architectural components and transaction types available on the network.

---

## Network Participants

The core infrastructure relies on two main types of entities interacting on the network.

### Institutions

An institution is any regulated entity moving money on behalf of its customers.

- **What they do:** They register their own top-level domains (like `@scbank` or `@momo`), issue user handles, perform local compliance (KYC/AML), and maintain a settlement balance with Zelkea.
- **When to use:** When you are a licensed financial entity wanting to grant your existing user base global financial interoperability.

### Businesses

Payment processors, merchants, SaaS platforms, and marketplaces that want to collect funds from customers globally.

- **What they do:** Integrate the Zelkea API into their checkout flows to receive payments instantly under a business handle marked with their country's domain (like `netflix@us` or `shop@jp`)
- **When to use:** When you need a single integration to accept global, multi-currency payments without maintaining separate payment gateways or managing payouts.

---

## Addressing Layer

### Handles

The secure, human-readable identifiers (`user@domain`) that replace sensitive banking information across the network.

- **What they do:** They act as a universal routing address. Zelkea maps the handle to the corresponding institution's balance to settle transactions without ever exposing bank account numbers, card details, or wallet identifiers.
- **When to use:** Used to send or receive money without sharing sensitive financial details.

---

## Transaction Types

Zelkea instantly processes transactions globally by clearing funds directly between the balances of participating institutions and platforms.

| Product | Description | Use when you need to... |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Transfers** | Direct account-to-account transaction between a sender handle and a receiver handle. | Send or receive money between two specific accounts (e.g., P2P remittances or bank-to-bank transfers). |
| **Payments** | Dynamic checkout flows including one-time charges, recurring subscriptions, or marketplace splits using single-use customer authorization codes. | Build merchant checkout experiences, automate subscription billing, or split a single customer payment among multiple parties simultaneously. |
