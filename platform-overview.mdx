title: "Platform overview"
---

Zelkea is a financial interoperability network that enables institutions and businesses to move money using short and memorable identifier called handles.

This guide introduces the core components of the network and how money moves through it.

## Network participants

The core infrastructure relies on two main types of entities interacting on the network.

### Institutions

An institution is a regulated entity moving money on behalf of its customers.

- **What they do:** They register a memorable domain (like `@scbank` or `@payme`), issue handles to their customers, perform local compliance (KYC/AML), and maintain a pre-funded balance with Zelkea.
- **When to use:** When you are a licensed financial entity wanting to grant your existing user base financial interoperability.

### Businesses

Payment processors, merchants, SaaS platforms, and marketplaces that want to collect funds from customers globally.

- **What they do:** Integrate the Zelkea API into their checkout flows to receive payments instantly under a business handle marked with their country's domain (like `company@us` or `shop@jp`)
- **When to use:** When you need a single integration to accept global, multi-currency payments without maintaining separate local payment gateways.

## Handles

The secure, human-readable identifiers (`customer@domain`) that replace long and sensitive banking information across the network.

- **What they do:** They act as a universal routing address. Zelkea maps the handle to the corresponding institution's balance to settle transactions without ever exposing personal information, bank account numbers, card details, or wallet identifiers.
- **When to use:** Used to make online payments and send or receive money without sharing sensitive financial details.

## Transaction types

Zelkea instantly processes transactions globally by clearing funds directly between the balances of participating institutions and platforms.

| Product | Description | Use when you need to... |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Transfers** | Direct account-to-account transaction between a sender handle and a receiver handle. | Send or receive money between two specific accounts (e.g., P2P remittances or bank-to-wallet transfers). |
| **Payments** | Dynamic checkout flows including one-time charges, recurring subscriptions, or marketplace splits. | Build merchant checkout experiences, automate subscription billing, or split a single customer payment among multiple parties simultaneously. |
