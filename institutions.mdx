---
title: "Institutions"
description: "Banks and fintechs that issue handles and move money on behalf of their users."
---

An institution is a regulated entity that already serves its own users, such as a bank, mobile money operator, or neobank. By joining Zelkea, those users become reachable across the entire network without the institution having to build separate payment connections with every other institution.

<Note>
  If you already run KYC/AML and hold accounts for real people, you're an institution. If you're accepting payments for goods or services and don't hold customer deposits yourself, you probably are a [Business](/businesses) instead.
</Note>

## What an institution actually does

- **Owns a domain.** Registered once, at onboarding — for example `@mpesa` or `@scbank`. Every handle your institution issues lives under it.
- **Issues handles to its own users.** You decide who gets one, what their starting balance and limits are, and when to freeze or update them.
- **Does the compliance work.** KYC, AML, sanctions screening — all of that stays with you, the same as it does today. Zelkea never becomes the entity holding that relationship.
- **Keeps its own ledger.** Zelkea tells you the moment money moves, in real time, over webhooks. Updating your internal balance in response to that event is on you — Zelkea doesn't hold a balance on your behalf.

## What you get in return

Every handle you issue can send and receive money from **every other institution and business on the network**, instantly, without you integrating anything else. One relationship with Zelkea replaces N one-off integrations with N different banks and wallets.

## A day in the life of an institution

```bash Register your first handle
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/handles' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "handle": "malick",
    "balance": 1000,
    "currency": "USD",
    "limit": 5000,
    "code": "XSO2B9",
    "status": "active"
  }'
```

```json Response
{
  "handle": "malick@wallet",
  "balance": 1000,
  "currency": "USD",
  "limit": 5000,
  "status": "active",
  "created_at": "2026-07-10T00:21:49Z"
}
```

From this point, `malick@wallet` can send and receive from any handle on the network, and you'll get a webhook every time money touches his handle so your own ledger stays in sync.

## Keeping your ledger in sync

Since Zelkea doesn't store a running balance for you, two things matter:

1. **Listen for webhooks.** Every transfer or payment involving one of your handles fires a `transfer.completed` or `payment.completed` event. This is how you find out money moved.
2. **Update your own balance field.** When you issue or adjust a handle, `balance` reflects what you tell Zelkea it is — it's used for spend-limit checks on outbound transfers, not treated as Zelkea's own record of truth.

<Tip>
  Think of `balance` and `limit` on a handle the same way you'd think of a spend cap you hand to a card processor — it's a control you set, not a balance Zelkea manages for you.
</Tip>

## Common use cases

- A mobile money operator making its users reachable by banks in other countries, without a correspondent banking relationship.
- A neobank letting customers pay any business on the network directly from their app balance.
- A licensed remittance company routing cross-border transfers without holding pre-funded balances in every corridor.
