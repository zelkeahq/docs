A business is any company that gets paid — a merchant, a SaaS platform, a marketplace — but doesn't hold customer deposits itself. If you're building a checkout, not a bank account, this is you.

<Note>
  Businesses use the [Payments API](/payments) to collect money. They can't move money directly between two other handles — that's reserved for [Institutions](/institutions), since it requires holding the compliance relationship with the sender.
</Note>

## What a business actually does

- **Gets a handle under its country's domain.** For example `company@us` or `shop@jp`. This is what customers pay to — no merchant account, no gateway credentials, no PCI scope.
- **Collects payments with a customer's handle and a one-time code.** The customer generates the code on their institution's app; you never see or store anything more sensitive than that.
- **Gets paid instantly, in the currency you registered in.** No 2-3 day settlement delay, no payout batching.

## Collecting a payment

Say a customer wants to pay `company@us` \$14.99 using their `malick@wallet` handle. Malick opens his wallet app, generates a one-time code, and gives it to you at checkout — the same motion as typing a card CVV, just without the card.

```bash Charge a customer
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 7b9e4a3c-2d1e-4f5a-8b6c-9d8e7f6a5b4c' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "customer": "malick@wallet",
    "amount": 14.99,
    "currency": "USD",
    "code": "7X9K2M"
  }'
```

```json Response
{
  "id": "pmt_a1b2c3d4",
  "merchant": "company@us",
  "customer": "malick@wallet",
  "amount": 14.99,
  "currency": "USD",
  "status": "completed",
  "created_at": "2026-07-10T00:22:00Z"
}
```

The code is single-use — it's rotated automatically the moment it's spent, so it can't be replayed even if it leaks.

## Splitting a payment across multiple recipients

If you're a marketplace, you don't have to collect the full amount yourself and pay out separately. Splits happen inside the same transaction:

```json Split example
{
  "customer": "malick@wallet",
  "amount": 100,
  "currency": "USD",
  "code": "7X9K2M",
  "splits": [
    { "handle": "vendor1@us", "amount": 70 },
    { "handle": "vendor2@us", "amount": 30 }
  ]
}
```

Both vendors receive their share instantly and directly — you never hold the full \$100 and push it back out yourself.

## Getting paid means knowing you got paid

Poll for it, or get told the instant it happens:

```bash List payments received
curl --request GET 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY"
```

Or better — [register a webhook](/webhooks) once, and get a `payment.completed` event pushed to you the moment any payment lands, whether it came through your checkout flow or as one leg of someone else's split.

## Common use cases

- A subscription SaaS billing customers directly from their bank or mobile wallet balance, no card required.
- A marketplace splitting a single checkout across several sellers in one transaction.
- A physical retailer accepting payment with nothing but a handle and a code — no POS terminal, no card reader.
