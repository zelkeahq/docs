title: "Businesses"
---

A business is any company that receives payments, such as a merchant, SaaS platform, or marketplace. If you are building a checkout experience or payment flow rather than a financial product, this is the right model for you.

## What a business actually does

- **Gets a handle under its country's domain.** For example, `company@us` or `shop@jp`. It works like a standard financial handle issued by an institution. Customers send payments directly to this handle, without merchant accounts, gateway credentials, or PCI scope.
- **Accepts online payments.** The customer generates a one-time payment code from their institution's app and uses it at checkout. The code expires after the payment attempt.
- **Receives funds instantly.** No settlement delays, no payout batching, and no waiting days for funds to become available.

## Collecting a payment

Say a customer wants to pay `company@us` \$14.99 using their `malick@wallet` handle. Malick opens his wallet app, copy his one-time payment code, and gives it to you at checkout — the same motion as typing a card CVV, just without the card.

```bash Charge a customer
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header 'Authorization: Bearer <API key>' \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 7b9e4a3c-2d1e-4f5a-8b6c-9d8e7f6a5b4c' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "customer": "malick@wallet",
    "amount": 14.99,
    "currency": "USD",
    "code": "7X9K2M"
  }'
```

```json Response
{
  "id": "pmt_a1b2c3d4",
  "merchant": "company@us",
  "customer": "malick@wallet",
  "amount": 14.99,
  "currency": "USD",
  "status": "completed",
  "created_at": "2026-07-10T00:22:00Z"
}
```

The code is single-use and automatically rotated after each payment attempt, preventing it from being reused even if exposed. After it is used, the code has no remaining value and can be safely stored if needed.

## Splitting a payment across multiple recipients

If you're a marketplace, you don't have to collect the full amount yourself and pay out separately. Splits happen inside the same transaction:

```json Split example
{
  "customer": "malick@wallet",
  "amount": 100,
  "currency": "USD",
  "code": "7X9K2M",
  "splits": [
    { "handle": "vendor1@au", "amount": 70 },
    { "handle": "company@us", "amount": 30 }
  ]
}
```

Both you and the seller or creator receive your share instantly. You never hold the full \$100, and there is no need to manage separate payouts.

## Getting paid means knowing you got paid

Poll for it, or get told the instant it happens:

```bash List payments received
curl --request GET 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header 'Authorization: Bearer <API key>'
```

Or better — [register a webhook](/webhooks) once, and get a `payment.completed` event pushed to you the moment any payment lands, whether it came through your checkout flow or as one leg of someone else's split.

## Common use cases

- A subscription SaaS billing customers directly from their bank or mobile wallet balance, no card required.
- A marketplace splitting a single checkout across several sellers in one transaction.
- An online retailer accepting payment with nothing but a handle and a code.
