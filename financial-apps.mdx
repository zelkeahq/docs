If you're building a neobank, a savings app, or any product where users hold a balance and move money in and out of it, you're an [institution](/institutions) — even if you're small, even if you haven't got a banking license yet and are operating under a partner bank's.

The problem this solves: normally, launching in a new country means a new integration — a new mobile money API here, a new local bank rail there, each with its own auth, its own quirks, its own support team to build a relationship with. Zelkea replaces all of that with one integration that already reaches every other institution on the network.

## What this actually looks like

Your app issues a handle to every user the moment they sign up. From then on, that handle is how they send, receive, and get paid — inside your app, but also from anyone else on the network.

```bash Issue a handle when a user signs up
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/handles' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "handle": "sara.k",
    "balance": 0,
    "currency": "USD",
    "limit": 2000,
    "code": "9F3K7Q",
    "status": "active"
  }'
```

Now `sara.k@yourapp` exists on the network. She can receive a salary payment from a business, send money to a friend at a different bank, or pay a merchant — all without you building a single extra integration.

## Letting users send money

A "send to a friend" button in your app is a transfer call, nothing more:

```bash Send money to another handle
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 3c2d1e4f-5a8b-6c9d-8e7f-6a5b4c7b9e4a' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "sender": "sara.k@yourapp",
    "receiver": "malick@wallet",
    "amount": 50,
    "currency": "USD"
  }'
```

Sara never needed Malick's bank details, and you never needed to know M-Pesa's API — the handle carried all of that.

## Keeping balances accurate in real time

Since Zelkea doesn't hold a ledger for you, your app's job is to listen and react:

```bash Register a webhook once
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/webhooks' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "url": "https://yourapp.com/webhooks/zelkea"
  }'
```

Every `transfer.completed` or `payment.completed` event that touches one of your users' handles lands here the instant it happens — that's your signal to update the balance shown in your app.

## Common patterns

- **Savings app** — hold user balances, let them move money in from any institution without a separate deposit flow.
- **Payroll app** — pay out salaries as transfers directly to each employee's handle, wherever they bank.
- **Multi-currency wallet** — one handle, funded and spent across borders without opening a local account in each country.
