If you're building a neobank, a payments app, or any product where users hold a balance and move money in and out of it, you're an institution, even if you're small, don't yet have a banking license, and operate under a partner bank's.

Normally, launching in a new country means a new integration, a new mobile money API here, a new local bank rail there, each with its own concepts, documentation, quirks, and support team to manage. Zelkea replaces all of that with one integration that already reaches every other institution on the network.

## What this actually looks like

Your app issues each user a handle the moment they sign up. From then on, that handle becomes their way to send and receive money, and make online and offline payments.

```bash Issue a handle when a user signs up
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/handles' \
  --header 'Authorization: Bearer <API key>' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "handle": "sara.k",
    "balance": 0,
    "currency": "USD",
    "limit": 2000,
    "code": "9F3K7Q",
    "status": "active"
  }'
```

Now `sara.k@yourapp` exists on the network. She can receive salary payments from a business, send money to a friend at another bank, or pay a merchant, all without you building any additional integrations.

## Letting users send money

A "send money" button in your app is a transfer call, nothing more:

```bash Send money to another handle
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers' \
  --header 'Authorization: Bearer <API key>' \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 3c2d1e4f-5a8b-6c9d-8e7f-6a5b4c7b9e4a' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "sender": "sara.k@yourapp",
    "receiver": "malick@wallet",
    "amount": 50,
    "currency": "USD"
  }'
```

Sara does not need to know where Malick banks, his account number, or any personal information such as his full name, phone number, or address. She does not need access to any sensitive details. All she needs is Malick’s handle.

## Keeping balances accurate in real time

Since Zelkea doesn't hold a ledger for you, your app's job is to listen and react:

```bash Register a webhook once
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/webhooks' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "url": "https://yourapp.com/webhooks/zelkea"
  }'
```

Every `transfer.completed` or `payment.completed` event that touches one of your users' handles lands here the instant it happens — that's your signal to update the balance shown in your app.

## Common patterns

- **Savings app** — hold user balances, let them move money in from any institution without a separate deposit flow.
- **Payroll app** — pay out salaries as transfers directly to each employee's handle, wherever they bank.
- **Multi-currency wallet** — one handle, funded and spent across borders without opening a local account in each country.
