---
title: "Transfers"
description: "Move money directly from one handle to another."
---

A transfer is the simplest thing you can do on Zelkea: take money from one handle, put it in another. No checkout, no customer authorization code, no splits — just a sender, a receiver, and an amount.

<Note>
  Transfers are for **institutions** moving money on behalf of, or between, handles they're authorized to act for. If you're a business trying to get paid by a customer, you want [Payments](/payments) instead.
</Note>

## When to use a transfer

- A customer at your institution sending money to a friend at a different bank.
- Settling a cross-border remittance corridor between two institutions.
- Moving money between two handles under your own domain.

## Sending one

Every transfer needs a unique `idempotency-key` — a UUID you generate yourself. If your request times out and you retry with the same key, Zelkea returns the original result instead of moving the money twice.

```bash Send a transfer
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 7b9e4a3c-2d1e-4f5a-8b6c-9d8e7f6a5b4c' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "sender": "alice@acme",
    "receiver": "bob@scbank",
    "amount": 250,
    "currency": "USD"
  }'
```

```json Response
{
  "id": "tx_987654321",
  "sender": "alice@acme",
  "receiver": "bob@scbank",
  "amount": 250,
  "currency": "USD",
  "status": "completed",
  "created_at": "2026-07-10T00:22:00Z",
  "reference": "invoice-4471"
}
```

`reference` is optional and entirely yours — pass your own internal ID, invoice number, or ticket reference, and it comes back untouched on every read of this transfer. It's how you tie a Zelkea transaction back to your own records without maintaining a separate mapping table.

<Warning>
  Transfers settle instantly and are **final**. There's no reversal endpoint — Zelkea only routes money, it never holds a position to reverse from. If money needs to come back, that's a new transfer in the other direction, same as correcting a real bank wire.
</Warning>

## Looking one up later

Every transfer you send can be retrieved again, either as a list or by ID:

```bash Get transfer history for a handle
curl --request GET 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers/alice@acme' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY"
```

```bash Get one specific transfer
curl --request GET 'https://api.zelkea.com/transfers/alice@acme/tx_987654321' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY"
```

## Finding out the instant it happens

Don't poll for money movement — [register a webhook](/webhooks) once, and get a `transfer.completed` event pushed to both the sender's and receiver's institution the moment a transfer settles. This is what lets you keep your own ledger in sync without ever asking Zelkea "did anything change?"
