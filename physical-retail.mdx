---
title: "Physical retail"
description: "Accept in-person payments without a card reader, a POS terminal, or a percentage lost to card networks."
---

A physical retailer accepting Zelkea doesn't need any hardware. No card reader, no POS terminal, no monthly rental fee for a machine that breaks the week your busiest season starts. Just a [business handle](/businesses) and the customer's authorization code.

## What the transaction looks like at the counter

The customer opens their banking or wallet app, generates a one-time code, and reads it to the cashier — the same motion as reading out a CVV, except this code can't be reused even if someone overhears it.

```bash Charge the customer at checkout
curl --request POST 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY" \
  --header 'idempotency-key: 6a5b4c7b-9e4a-3c2d-1e4f-5a8b6c9d8e7f' \
  --header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  --data-raw '{
    "customer": "malick@mpesa",
    "amount": 8.50,
    "currency": "USD",
    "code": "4K7X2M"
  }'
```

```json Response
{
  "id": "pmt_r8t2v9",
  "merchant": "cornerstore@ke",
  "customer": "malick@mpesa",
  "amount": 8.50,
  "currency": "USD",
  "status": "completed",
  "created_at": "2026-07-10T09:12:44Z"
}
```

Money lands in the merchant's balance instantly — no next-day settlement, no batching, no waiting for a card network to release funds.

## Why this matters for a small business specifically

Card networks typically take 1.5-3.5% per transaction, plus a hardware rental fee, plus a payout delay of one to several days. None of that applies here:

- **No hardware** — a phone or a printed handle on a sign is enough to receive payment.
- **No delay** — money is usable the second it arrives, which matters when you're paying suppliers same-day.
- **No card-present risk** — there's no card number to skim, clone, or charge back fraudulently, because there was never a card.

## Handling a queue

Retail moves fast — a printed QR code encoding your handle at the register, paired with the customer typing their code, is often quicker than a chip-and-PIN transaction. If you're building the point-of-sale software yourself, `POST /payments` is the only call in the hot path — everything else (history, receipts, reconciliation) can happen after the customer has already walked away with their coffee.

## Reconciling at the end of the day

```bash Pull today's transactions
curl --request GET 'https://api.zelkea.com/payments' \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer $ZELKEA_API_KEY"
```

Or don't poll at all — [register a webhook](/webhooks) and have every sale pushed to your till system the instant it happens, so your end-of-day total is always already correct.
